CAESAR'S SUNDAY THOUGHT

Apparently, in life there are many things that don't fit in with our expectations of the ideal life. There are things we wish for, or work for, or pray for, and yet we don't get them. Bad things seem to happen to good people. The good guys get burnt (when the bad guys are apparently thriving!). We invest a lot in a relationship, and somehow, it doesn't work out. We trust, and we're heart broken. We work hard, and don't reap as much as we expected. We pray (and fast, and sow) and we seem not to receive the answers that we expect. We study hard and don't score as high. Or, may be, we seem to be paying for the sins of our parents...read more

This doesn't happen always, but sometimes. With a limited grasp of the workings, and goings on, of the Universe, we think that things ought to be different. We have no way of knowing that, actually, what we see as unfavorable in the present, may ultimately be for our good. We can't add things up looking forward. May be, when we look at them in retrospect, we'll know that they were all for our good (that may be in future, in time, or in eternity).

In the meantime, we need to maintain a positive, expectant, watchful, and prayerful attitude. An attitude where we're certain that with God, ultimately all things will work out for good. An attitude where we expect that God will surely intervene in our situations. An attitude where we're open and alert to the movement of the Spirit of God. When opportunity presents itself for your situation to change, don't be so complacent as to miss out on God's transformative work and intervention in your life. An attitude where we look out for the positive in every seemingly negative situation. An attitude where we affirm the truth about God (all knowing, perfectly good and loving, all powerful, all present).

May Jesus Christ visit you in a special way, and transform your situation for the glory of God. May your situation be so improved that people who knew you before will wonder whether it's true. May all things in your life work out for good.

JULIUS CAESAR kAMUKAMA
